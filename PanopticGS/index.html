PanopticGS: Language-Embedded Gaussian Splatting for Panoptic Scene Editing

Abstract

Current scene editing methodologies focus on either one-time appearance stylization or physical manipulation. In this study, we show that recent efforts in language grounding and instructive editing of neural-represented scenes can be synergized to achieve a more comprehensive, precise, and repeatable editing experience. We argue that a scene representation based on language-grounded geometric primitives, compared to an implicit radiance field, is more apt for instructive editing, as it provides effective anchors for grounding, positioning, and operation. In our approach, we utilize the 3D Gaussian representation to embed language cues through a differentiable rendering process. Upon receiving textual instruction, our system queries the targeted object using embedded 3D Gaussians, compared to an image-level querying in the previous method. For stylization-related editing, we implement multi-view consistent stylization guided by a single to multi-view diffuser, while for physical manipulations, we directly alter the geometric primitives in space. Experimental results demonstrate enhanced scene editability, enabling non-interfering style changes across objects and precise resizing, moving, deletion, or addition while preserving photorealism.
